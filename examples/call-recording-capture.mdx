This example shows how to extract actionable signals from call transcripts or meeting notes to continuously improve your context file, hypotheses, and email campaigns.

## Scenario

You just had a discovery call with a prospect (or won customer). The call transcript contains valuable signals about:
- Why they were interested (hypothesis validation)
- What language resonated
- Specific pain points they mentioned
- Objections or concerns
- Proof point opportunities

You want to capture these signals and update your context file.

## Starting Prompt

```
I just finished a call with a prospect.
Here's the transcript [paste transcript].
Extract signals and update my context file.
```

**Call Transcript:**

```
[2025-03-04 10:30 AM - Discovery Call]

Danny (Extruct): Thanks for taking the time. I saw you're building tenant 
screening features at PropertyFlow. What's the current workflow?

Sarah (PropertyFlow, Head of Product): Yeah, so our customers are property 
managers who need to evaluate potential tenants — mostly small businesses 
leasing commercial space. Right now they're doing this super manually.

Danny: What does "manually" mean?

Sarah: They Google the business name, check LinkedIn, maybe look at Yelp 
reviews. They're trying to figure out: is this business legit? Are they 
growing or shrinking? What industry are they in? The problem is this takes 
20-30 minutes per tenant, and our customers are evaluating 50+ tenants a month.

Danny: Got it. So it's a time sink, but also — is the data they find reliable?

Sarah: Exactly. Half the time they can't even find the company. Or they find 
some random LinkedIn profile but can't tell if it's the right company. We 
need a programmatic way to look up: company name → structured profile with 
headcount, industry, founding year, funding status, etc.

Danny: Makes sense. Are you building this yourself or looking for an API?

Sarah: We tried building it. Our engineers spent 3 weeks on scrapers. It 
sort of worked, but the data was super inconsistent and it kept breaking. 
We realized this isn't our core competency. We'd rather integrate an API 
if there's something reliable.

Danny: What about existing data providers? Clearbit, ZoomInfo?

Sarah: We looked at Clearbit. Two issues: (1) their coverage is mostly 
tech companies and larger businesses — we need smaller, local businesses too. 
(2) Their pricing is per-contact, but we don't need emails or people data, 
just company-level stuff. Felt like overkill.

Danny: Got it. So you need: SMB coverage, company-only data, API-first. 
What's the priority — speed, coverage, or cost?

Sarah: Coverage first. If we can't find the company, everything else is 
moot. But yeah, speed matters too — can't have our customers waiting 30 
seconds for a lookup.

Danny: And what happens if you can't solve this? Does the feature get 
deprioritized?

Sarah: Honestly, yes. We've already pushed the release twice. This is a 
top customer request, but if we can't get the data layer right, we'll have 
to kill it. That would suck because tenant screening is a key differentiator 
for us.

Danny: Makes sense. Let me show you Extruct — we're an API-first company 
intelligence platform. You can search by company name or domain and get 
back structured profiles: industry, headcount, location, funding, etc. 
Coverage is strong for SMBs, and response time is under 500ms for lookups.

Sarah: Interesting. What's the pricing model?

Danny: Search is free and unlimited. If you need enrichment beyond basic 
profiles — like custom research columns, deep company data — that's 
1 credit per cell, and plans start at $59/mo for 1K credits. But for 
tenant screening, you'd probably just use search, which is free.

Sarah: Wait, search is actually free? That's... surprising.

Danny: Yeah, we want developers to build on Extruct. Free search lowers 
the barrier. Most customers start with search, then add enrichment later 
when they need more depth.

Sarah: Okay, I'm interested. Can we test this with a sample of our tenant 
data? Maybe 50-100 companies?

Danny: Absolutely. I'll send you API access today. You can test search 
and see how coverage + speed feel for your use case.

Sarah: Perfect. One more question — do you have any other property tech 
customers? Just want to make sure this isn't a one-off.

Danny: We're working with a procurement automation platform that uses us 
for vendor scoring — similar workflow to tenant screening. And we're in 
conversations with two other property tech companies, but you'd be our 
first tenant screening customer.

Sarah: Cool, that's fine. Honestly, the fact that you have SMB coverage 
and free search is already way ahead of the other options we've looked at.

Danny: Great. I'll follow up with API docs and a sandbox key in the next hour.

Sarah: Sounds good. Talk soon.
```

## Key Takeaways

Call recording capture mode is valuable for:

1. **ICP refinement** — Discover new profiles (property tech) from real conversations
2. **Hypothesis validation** — Confirm which pain points resonate in discovery calls
3. **Proof point sourcing** — Capture quotes and metrics for future proof library entries
4. **Messaging optimization** — Learn what language and positioning works ("SMB coverage", "free tier")
5. **Continuous learning** — Every call improves your context file and campaign targeting

## Workflow Frequency

Run call recording capture after:

- Every demo or discovery call (during sales)
- Closed-won deals (capture full win case)
- Customer onboarding calls (learn actual use cases)
- Lost deals (capture objections and anti-patterns)

This creates a **feedback loop** where your outbound campaigns get smarter with every conversation.
